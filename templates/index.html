About

How Satellites work?

Sputnik 1

Sputnik 1 was the first artificial Earth satellite. The Soviet Union launched it into an elliptical low Earth orbit on 4 October 1957, orbiting for three weeks before its batteries died.

DaVinci

Teach students about radio waves, aeronautical engineering, space propulsion, and geography by sending a communication signal to schools around the world.

Polar

Jason 2

Monitor sea surface level and wave heights

Non-Polar Inclined

MicroDragon

Will monitor the world's oceans to examine fishing grounds and search for disasters such as oil spills; will also measure aerosols, microscopic particles suspended in Earth.

Sun-Synchronous

Kalpana-1 (Metsat-1)

Carries a VHRR (Very High Resolution Radiometer); Data Relay Transponder.

International Space Station

The ISS serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory in which crew members conduct experiments in biology, human biology, physics, astronomy, meteorology, and other fields.

Non-Polar Inclined

Odin

Originally two scientific disciplines on a single spacecraft in studies of star formation/early solar system (astronomy) and of the mechanisms behind the depletion of the ozone layer in the earth atmosphere and the effects of global warming (aeronomy); astronomy mission has ended.

Sun-Synchronous

Hubble Space Telescope

Hubble has an unobstructed view of the universe. Scientists have used Hubble to observe the most distant stars and galaxies as well as the planets in our solar system.The Hubble Space Telescope is named in honor of astronomer Edwin Hubble.

Non-Polar Inclined

Sapphire

Radar imaging satellite. Can measure moisture in soil to 2 meters.

Non-Polar Inclined

Seahawk-1

Will image the world's ocean for color (SOCON: Sustained Ocean Color Observations with Nanosatellites) to assess health. Prototype for a larger constellation.

Sun-Synchronous

SeeMe (Space Enabled Effects for Military Engagements satellite)

Real time situational awareness for troops on the ground with handheld devices. Possible constellation consisting of about 24 satellites.

Sun-Synchronous

OOREOS (OrganismOrganic Exposure to Orbital Stresses)

Carries two biological experiments - one will test live microorganisms, the other inanimate organic samples, to see how they react to conditions in space for six months.

Polar

INSAT 4A (Indian National Satellite)

12 Ku-band, 12 C-band transponders for data and TV services.
